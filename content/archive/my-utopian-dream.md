The current architecture of scientific progress is broken.
Most scientific knowledge is locked behind exorbitant paywalls, accessible only to institutions that can afford subscription fees.
Raw datasets—the literal building blocks of discovery—remain stored on private hard drives, forgotten or deliberately hidden.
At the same time, computing power is rapidly centralizing in the hands of a few tech conglomerates.

My utopian dream is a world where scientific discovery is decentralized, open, and collaborative by design.
A world where anyone, anywhere, can contribute to and benefit from the collective sum of human intelligence.

## The Pillar of Open Science

In this ideal future, the concept of a "journal subscription" is obsolete.
Every paper, experiment, and dataset is published to a global, immutable registry.
Instead of gatekeeping reviews, peer review is a continuous, community-driven process, incentivized through micro-grants or academic tokens.
When a researcher publishes a model, the weight files and training scripts are public, allowing anyone to replicate and build upon their findings.

## Decentralized Compute and Intelligence

As models grow larger, access to intelligence is dictated by hardware wealth.
A student in a developing country cannot train a state-of-the-art model because they lack access to high-end compute clusters.
In my utopian model, computing power is shared dynamically.
Idle consumer GPUs across the globe form a distributed, trustless network that runs simulations, trains scientific models, and computes equations for open-source research.

> "No single institution should own the substrate of human knowledge or the compute that refines it."

## How We Get There

It starts with the tools we build today.
Every time we choose to open-source a script, share a raw dataset, or host our code on open repositories instead of proprietary platforms, we take a step toward this dream.
We must build wrappers and APIs that make it easy for researchers to share their work with the click of a button.

It is a long road, but the alternative—a future where all scientific truth is owned by corporations and rented back to us—is too bleak to accept.
Let's build the open future together.
